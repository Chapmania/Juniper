8  /// The Universal Transverse Mercator (UTM) conformal projection uses a 2-dimensional Cartesian
9  /// coordinate system to give locations on the surface of the Earth. Like the traditional method
10  /// of latitude and longitude, it is a horizontal position representation, i.e. it is used to
11  /// identify locations on the Earth independently of vertical position. However, it differs from
12  /// that method in several respects. /// The UTM system is not a single map projection. The
13  /// system instead divides the Earth into sixty zones, each being a six-degree band of longitude,
