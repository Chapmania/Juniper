3  /// <summary>
4  /// The Universal Transverse Mercator (UTM) conformal projection uses a 2-dimensional Cartesian
5  /// coordinate system to give locations on the surface of the Earth. Like the traditional method
6  /// of latitude and longitude, it is a horizontal position representation, i.e. it is used to
7  /// identify locations on the Earth independently of vertical position. However, it differs from
8  /// that method in several respects. /// The UTM system is not a single map projection. The
9  /// system instead divides the Earth into sixty zones, each being a six-degree band of longitude,
10  /// and uses a secant transverse Mercator projection in each zone.
